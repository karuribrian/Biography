The first in-depth biography of Nipsey Hussle, the hip hop mogul, artist, and activist whose transformative legacy inspired a generation with his motivational lyrics and visionary business savvy—before he was tragically shot down in the very neighborhood he was dedicated to building up.

About the legend

He was born to an African American Mother and an Eritrean father in Los Angles.
He was known to sell mixtapes and was a solo artist.The 33year old Hussle was assassinated on 31st March 2019 when he was shot while leaving a clothing store he owned. His last tweet “Having strong enemies is a blessing spread a wave of curiousness and alarm among his fans.Hussle worked to stop gang violence because he was concerned about the rising number of death tolls among black men who lost their lives in criminal activities.

The first book to hit the market is “The Marathon Don’t Stop", The Life and Times of Nipsey Hussle. Written by the respected ex-Vibe magazine writer and editor Rob Kenner, the nearly 400-page biography sets itself a high bar: chronicle the artist’s life from cradle to grave — from his initiation into gang life to his sprawling mixtape catalog; from a three-month trip to his father’s native Eritrea to his fairy-tale union with the L.A. actor Lauren London. There are brief detours to explain redlining in the Crenshaw District and the historical brutality of the Los Angeles Police Department. Kenner has provided a thorough and well-researched history that hits the right notes and captures the essence of what made Hussle so significant.
